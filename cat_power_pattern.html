CAT5 / RJ45 Overview

Use T568B as the main field pattern here, but keep T568A on the page for reference. Male plug terminations and female keystone or jack terminations use the same pair scheme and pin numbers, but the physical termination method looks different.

Primary standard used here: T568B
Field reminder: pick one standard and stay consistent end to end unless you intentionally need a crossover result.

Female Jack / Keystone

Female jacks still follow T568A or T568B, but the wires are punched down onto the jack instead of inserted into a plug. Follow the color legend printed on the jack body and match the same standard on both ends.

Female Jack Using T568B

T568B: White/Orange, Orange, White/Green, Blue, White/Blue, Green, White/Brown, Brown

Female Jack Using T568A

T568A: White/Green, Green, White/Orange, Blue, White/Blue, Orange, White/Brown, Brown
Most keystones print both A and B patterns on the jack. Pick the correct side of the legend and stay consistent.

Equipment Layout Direction

This is the part that can trip people in the field. The pin functions stay the same, but the equipment face may show the numbers in opposite physical order.

Calix 716 / 717 ONT Cards

These are physically read right to left. The face appears as 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1. Even though the visible direction is reversed, the pin functions still follow the same 1 through 7 mapping listed above.
