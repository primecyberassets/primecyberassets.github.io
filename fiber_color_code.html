Fiber Color Code Cheat Sheet

A fast visual guide for the standard 12-fiber color order. Good for counting tubes, ribbons, and individual fibers without turning your brain into a spaghetti splice tray.

Standard 12-Color Order

1. Blue

Fiber 1

Starting point of the standard 12-color sequence.

2. Orange

Fiber 2

Second color in the normal fiber count order.

3. Green

Fiber 3

Common early-count color in tubes and ribbons.

4. Brown

5. Slate

Fiber 5

6. White

8. Black

Fiber 9

10. Violet

Remember It Fast

1 to 6
Blue, Orange, Green, Brown, Slate, White
7 to 12
Red, Black, Yellow, Violet, Rose, Aqua
Field shorthand
Slate often means gray. Rose often means pink. Violet often gets called purple.

How It Repeats

The same 12-color order is reused again and again in fiber work. That means:

  • Loose tube counts often repeat the standard 12-color sequence.
  • Ribbon fiber positions usually follow the same 12-color order.
  • Higher counts build from the same base pattern instead of inventing a new rainbow every time.

Common Uses

  • Identifying fiber position inside a 12-fiber tube
  • Checking ribbon fiber order during splicing
  • Verifying counts when moving between trays and panels
  • Cross-checking tube, ribbon, and fiber math with your calculator page
